# English Pitch Deck - 12-Slide Content Draft

## Slide 1 - The Grain of Tomorrow

**Headline:** The Grain of Tomorrow  
**Subline:** An AI-assisted early-warning platform for rice-growing risk management.

**On-slide:**

- Does not replace labs.
- Does not ban farming.
- Helps test the right places first.

**Speaker note:**  
The Grain of Tomorrow starts with arsenic risk in rice, but the broader goal is to help rice-growing areas manage environmental and food-chain risks with data and AI.

## Slide 2 - The Problem

**Headline:** Some growing-area risks cannot be seen by eye.

**On-slide:**

- Rice is a daily staple.
- Environmental risks may accumulate over time.
- Sampling resources are limited.
- Decision-makers need to know where to check first.

**Speaker note:**  
We are not trying to create fear around rice. We are solving a practical monitoring problem: when testing resources are limited, where should we prioritize sampling?

## Slide 3 - Why Arsenic?

**Headline:** Arsenic is not an acute scare; it is a long-term monitoring and quality-management risk.

**On-slide:**

- Invisible to consumers and farmers.
- Related to soil, irrigation water and cultivation conditions.
- Relevant to sourcing-area trust and export quality management.

**Speaker note:**  
If people ask why they should care when they have eaten rice for years, the answer is that this is not about immediate poisoning. It is about long-term risk management and better targeted testing.

## Slide 4 - Our Solution

**Headline:** AI helps prioritize where sampling should happen first.

**On-slide:**

- Early-warning risk map.
- Uncertainty-aware prediction.
- Role-based chatbot explanations.
- Toolkit for sampling and risk communication.

**Speaker note:**  
The system does not certify whether an area is safe or unsafe. It gives an early signal so cooperatives, buyers and local agencies can decide where to test first.

## Slide 5 - Prototype Workflow

**Headline:** From AI signal to real-world action.

**On-slide workflow:**

1. Select a scenario and rice-growing area.
2. View risk signals and uncertainty bands.
3. Ask the chatbot for role-specific explanations.
4. Export Partner Pro sourcing-area reports.
5. Use lab testing for official confirmation.

**Speaker note:**  
The prototype is not just a map. It connects the dashboard, chatbot, toolkit and Partner Pro reporting flow.

## Slide 6 - Technology

**Headline:** Transparent predictions with uncertainty and explanation.

**On-slide:**

- Gaussian Process Regression for prediction.
- p10/p50/p90 uncertainty bands.
- SHAP for feature influence.
- RAG chatbot grounded in project context.

**Speaker note:**  
We do not hide uncertainty. Uncertainty is exactly why the system recommends additional sampling in priority areas.

## Slide 7 - Users And Beneficiaries

**Headline:** Direct users and final beneficiaries are not always the same group.

**On-slide:**

- Direct users: cooperatives, buyers/exporters, extension officers, researchers and labs.
- Beneficiaries: farmers, cooperatives, local agencies and consumers.
- Payers/sponsors: Partner Pro organizations, regional sponsors and CSR/ESG programs.

**Speaker note:**  
Farmers are central beneficiaries, but they are not the primary paying customer and should not be forced to use a complex dashboard directly.

## Slide 8 - Assisted Access For Farmers

**Headline:** Farmers should not be forced to read AI dashboards.

**On-slide:**

- Information is delivered through cooperatives, extension officers, Zalo groups, workshops and checklists.
- Risk messages are translated into simple actions: monitor, sample, verify with labs.
- Avoid labeling an entire area as risky without verification data.

**Speaker note:**  
Even when farmers have limited access to technology, the system can still support the people and organizations that work with them.

## Slide 9 - Financial Model

**Headline:** The pilot can sustain itself with five Partner Pro accounts.

**On-slide:**

- 12-month operating budget: 75M VND.
- Partner Pro: 15M VND/year/organization.
- Break-even: 5 organizations x 15M VND = 75M VND.
- Regional sponsorship: 50M VND/year.

**Speaker note:**  
The public version remains free for social impact. Paid value comes from sourcing-area reports, data export, organization quotas and pilot support.

## Slide 10 - 12-Month Pilot Plan

**Headline:** Start focused, measure impact, then expand.

**On-slide:**

- Months 1-2: stakeholder interviews and pilot-area validation.
- Months 3-5: dashboard and toolkit pilot with 5-10 partners.
- Months 6-8: standardize Pro reports and optimize API cost.
- Months 9-12: convert Partner Pro users and expand indicators.

**Speaker note:**  
We are not claiming to become a national system immediately. The first realistic scope is a Red River Delta pilot.

## Slide 11 - Impact

**Headline:** Impact means testing the right places earlier.

**On-slide:**

- Scientists: uncertainty and SHAP views for better sampling design.
- Local agencies: dashboard for monitoring and resource allocation.
- Cooperatives and buyers: sourcing-area risk management.
- Farmers: early guidance through familiar local channels.

**Speaker note:**  
The social impact is not about replacing people. It is about helping stakeholders coordinate before risks become post-harvest problems.

## Slide 12 - The Ask

**Headline:** Help us test the right places and act earlier.

**On-slide:**

- 5-10 pilot partners.
- Lab and research validation partners.
- One regional pilot sponsor.

**Speaker note:**  
After the competition, the priority is not rapid expansion. It is real-world validation, partner onboarding and sustaining the first 12 months.
